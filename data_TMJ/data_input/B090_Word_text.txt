Left TMJ coronal view shows a condyle that shows some decreased vertical and particularly lateral joint space. On both sides, the lateral rim of the fossa curves inferiorly about 3-4 mm. Basic diagnoses for B090 would be as follows:

Bilateral TMJ mild arthritis and articular discs probably medially displaced. TMJ pain, being worse on the right side. Occasional headache.
Myofascial pain affecting predominantly the right masseter, left SCM, and left posterior neck.

Recommended treatment would be as follows:
MRI of left and right TMJ. Consider TMJ bite splint to unload the joint some.
Surgery option 1.
Consider bilateral TMJ articular disc repositioning and ligament repair with Mitek anchor if the discs are identified as being displaced on the MRI.
Bilateral mandibular ramus sagittal split osteotomies to maintain a Class I occlusion, but create space within the joint to accept the articular disc. A second surgical option if the discs are displaced and nonsalvageable would include the following:
Bilateral TMJ reconstruction with TMJ Concepts total joint prostheses. Bilateral TMJ fat grafts (harvest from the abdomen).

I believe either of these treatment plans could work very well for her depending on what the MRI assessment reveals.
