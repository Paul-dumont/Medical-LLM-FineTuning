CLINICAL EXAMINATION

In frontal view, B490 has good left to right facial symmetry. Upper lip length is about 20 mm. Upper tooth-to-lip relationship is about 2 mm. Maxillary midline, mandibular dental midline and chin are all fairly well aligned with each other. B490 has multiple diastemas around her upper anterior teeth extending from the cuspid through cuspid in the upper arch. In profile view, B490 has good facial balance. Intraorally, she has all of her lower teeth back through her third molars present. She appears to have adequate attached gingiva around the lower anterior teeth although the roots are quite prominent labially and the crowns appear to be slightly lingually inclined. A little bit of spacing between some of the teeth. In the upper arch she has all of her teeth back through the third molars present. She is basically a Class I cuspid relationship, and maybe just slightly a Class II on the left. She has an open bit and appears to occlude only on her third molars and all other teeth appear to be out of occlusion. She has about 1 mm vertical overlap with her anterior teeth. Open bite may be slightly worse on the left side as compared to the right side. The left side open bite may be a good 2 to 3 mm and on the right side it may be 1 to 2 mm. The oropharyngeal tissues look normal and healthy. Intranasally, the septum is deviated off the left side anteriorly. The left turbinate is moderately enlarged. The right turbinate is fairly small at least anteriorly. Airway looks reasonably unobstructed. B490 does report some mild snoring and some mild daytime somnolence but no sleep apnea. She also reports a mild sleep disorder.  

To palpation, B490 has some mild to moderate tenderness over the TMJs and some mild tenderness over the masseters, medial pterygoids, styloid areas, SCMs, posterior neck and shoulder areas. On opening she has a click at about 10 mm and a closing click about 5 mm. The right side of the click is much more palpable and louder than the left side is. Maximal incisal opening is 50 mm but only about 29 without discomfort. Excursive movement to the right 7 mm, left 8 mm.

RADIOGRAPHIC EVALUATION
:
Panorex shows the presence of all of her teeth. She has root canals on the maxillary lateral incisors and left central incisor as well as the mandibular central incisors. The sinuses look relatively clear. Difficult to evaluate the TMJs.

Waters view shows that the bony septum is very straight and the turbinates are moderate in size. There seems to be a little bite more obstruction on the right side than the left side at least towards the posterior aspect.

Tomogram of the left TMJ shows increased vertical joint space and posterior joint space probably related to the splint therapy. Articular eminence is moderately steep. The patient may not have been in centric relation when this x-ray was taken.

Right TMJ tomogram shows a better balanced joint space or slightly decreased joint space. Articular eminence is moderately steep. On opening, there is good translation of both condyles.

Lateral cephalometric radiograph shows that she occludes only on the second molars and the rest of the teeth are all out of occlusion. She has a slight increased occlusal plane angle and the mandible. Oropharyngeal airway is somewhat constricted particularly at the soft palate area where the AP dimension is only about 5 to 6 mm. At the base of the tongue the AP dimension is about 7 mm. The tongue, however, is held down from the roof of the mouth. B490 has a tooth size discrepancy with a Bolton's difference of 1.5 mm. However, hand articulating the models seem to indicate that the discrepancy is much larger.

Nocturnal clenching and bruxism. Multiple diastemas around the upper teeth. Tooth size discrepancy.
