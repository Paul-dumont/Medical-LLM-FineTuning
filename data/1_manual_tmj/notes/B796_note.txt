Patient History

Bre’s TMJ problems may have begun about three years ago. She remembers about three years ago she got hit in the face with a medicine ball that sort of knocked her out for a little while. About two years ago, someone was carrying her and dropped her, and she struck her chin on a countertop and got bruised. She also had her wisdom teeth removed in 11/2001. Bre was born with congenitally missing the maxillary lateral incisors. From ’98 to 2000, she went through orthodontics to open up spacing for those lateral incisors and had a bridge to replace the missing laterals. In 2002 through 2003, she was re-treated by Dr. Mike Steffen, who had the upper bridges removed and opened up spaces. Bre subsequently has had dental implants placed in the maxillary lateral incisor areas and has had crowns placed on those. She has had some chiropractic treatment. She has had some problems with some other joints in the past but has no problems now with any other joints except for her jaw joints. 

Bre gets headaches about two to three times a week that she rates at about a level of 7 that occur predominantly in the frontal and temporal area. She rates her daily TMJ pain at about a 7, but again it can escalate to 10. Her average daily pain is about a 6. She rates her jaw function at 6, diet at 7, and disability at 4. 

She gets occasional earaches, occasional tinnitus, and occasional vertigo but no hearing loss. No other joints bother her at this time. Bre has worn a bite splint to try to deprogram her muscles, but this was ineffective for her. She wore it for about a week, but it made her pain more intense. 

In searching further into previous traumatic injuries to the jaws, Bre stated that the first injury with the medicine ball occurred when some people were playing with a medicine ball, and she happened to be walking by and got struck on the left side of the face. She apparently blacked out or was unconscious or a few minutes after that incident. She had some significant pain and discomfort around the head and jaw area for a few days. It then eventually subsided. The second incident occurred about two years ago where some boys picked her up and dropped her where her chin struck a countertop, and she got quite bruised around the jaws after that. It was after this incident that her pain began to worsen significantly. She was first diagnosed with the TMJ problems by Dr. Mike Steffen approximately two years ago. This was after both of those traumatic injuries to the jaws.
