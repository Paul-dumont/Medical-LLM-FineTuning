CALE RICH									10/24/2011

Cale is 22 years old, birth date 11/02/1988.  Referred by Dr. Ronald Auvenshine, 505 S. Main Street, Suite 210, Houston, TX        .

Cale believes that he injured his jaw when he was 19 years old playing soccer when he slipped and his chin came down and struck his knee.  He has had some difficulties with his jaw since that time.  He saw Dr. Auvenshine and has been treated by him for the past 2 years and predominantly has been treated with splint therapy.  This has helped Cale, but has not eliminated his pain issues. 

Cale gets headaches in the left side in the frontal and temporal area at about a level of 8.  He gets these headaches daily.  He rates his TMJ pain on the left side only at a level of 8 and his average daily pain around the head and neck area as a level of 8.  He rates his jaw function and diet at 0 but disability at 8.  It interferes significantly with what he is able to do because of the pain factors.  He has no problems with earaches, but does has continuous tinnitus on the left side and frequent vertigo.  He has no hearing loss and no other joints bother him.  He is not aware of clenching or bruxing.  He takes Aleve 2 tablets b.i.d. and Flexeril 10 mg q.h.s.

In frontal view, Cale has good facial symmetry and balance.  The maxillary, mandibular and chin midline are all pretty much  aligned with each other.  In profile, he has adequate facial balance, although some slight AP retrusion of the chin area.  Intraorally, Cale has all of his lower teeth back through the second molars.  The teeth are fairly well aligned and leveled.  In the upper arch, he also has all of his teeth back through the second molars.  He has a Class I cuspid-molar relationship with an open bite on the left side of about 1 to 2 mm ___________________________.  On the right side, his teeth do touch.  He has a 2 mm vertical over bite relationship.  As Cale opens, the mandible deviates first off to the left side and then it opens better and then shifts a little bit towards to the right.  To palpation, he has high moderate pain over the left masseter, medial pterygoid, TMJ and retromandibular area and mild to moderate pain over the left SCM, posterior neck and shoulder area.  He has no pain to palpation on the right side.  Maximal incisal opening is 60 mm, excursion movement to the right 8 mm and to the left 10 mm.  

Basic diagnoses would be as follows:

Left TMJ articular disc dislocation.
Left mandibular relative hyperplasia.
Left TMJ severe pain.
Left-sided myofascial pain.

Recommended surgical treatment would be as follows:

Left TMJ articular disc repositioning and ligament repair with Mitek anchor.
Left mandibular ramus osteotomy to allow the ramus and condyle to move down and forward to create space to accommodate the articular disc.
Temporary application of maxillary and mandibular arch bars.
