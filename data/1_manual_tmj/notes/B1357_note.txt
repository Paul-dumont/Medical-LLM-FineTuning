Upper lip length is about 23 mm.  Upper tooth lip relationship is about zero.  The maxillary dental midline is pretty much on the facial midline.  The mandibular dental midline also fairly close to being on the facial midline about 6 to 7 mm.   In profile view, the nasal tip has greater projection than the dorsum area.  The domes are quite prominent and the skin is very thin over the alar domes.  She is slightly retruded in the subnasale about 2 to 3 mm.  The upper lip itself at the vermilion area is slightly prominent.  Chin neck distance is quite short, although the chin neck angle is reasonably well defined. The mandible and chin appear to be significantly retruded.  The chin may be retruded by approximately 12 mm.  Intraorally, Tracy has all of her lower teeth back through the third molars. She has a lingual tori of moderate size on the left side.  In the upper arch, Tracy has all of her teeth back through the second molars present.  She has a Class I cuspid molar relationship on the right side as well as on the left side.  She has an anterior deep bite of about 4 mm. Maximal opening is 49 mm but only about 23 mm without pain.  Excursion movement to the left 9 mm and to the right 7 mm, but painful.  As she opens, she deviates to the left side approximately 3 mm.  To palpation, she has high moderate tenderness over the masseters, retromandibular areas, and left TMJ and moderate tenderness over the SCMs and medial pterygoids.  On opening, she has a click in the right side at about 20 mm and a closing click at 10 mm. Waters view shows that the nasal septum is reasonably straight.  The right turbinate appears quite large and may be blocking the right nasal airway.  The left side appears more open.  Sinuses appear relatively clear.  Asymmetry in the mandible can be seen with the left angle of the mandible being almost 1 cm higher than on the right side. 
Right TMJ tomogram shows that the morphology of the condyle appears fairly normal.  Articular eminence is moderately steep.   Joint space appears fairly well balanced.
Left TMJ tomogram shows a condyle that is severely atrophic with loss of vertical and anteroposterior volume.  There is a decreased posterior and to a lesser extent superior joint space.  Articular eminence is moderately steep.  On opening, there is excellent translation of the right condyle and some restricted translation of the left condyle.  The joint space significantly decreases in translation on the left side.  
Lateral cephalometric radiograph shows that the maxilla and mandible are moderately retruded.  Vertical height of the mandible on the left side is about almost 9 mm short of vertically than the right side. Tracy has significant difficulties swallowing and this has been going on since her TMJ problems developed.  She cannot swallow tablets and has to chew them or crush them in order to get them down.  Tracy has had Restylane injections to the upper lip and has made it more full.  This was 2 years ago and has not resolved away as much as expected.

Mandibular AP hypoplasia and asymmetry.
Class I malocclusion.  
Transverse cant in the arches with the left side being 3 mm vertically short compared to the right side.  
Crowding in the lower anterior arch. 
Lingual inclination of the mandibular left posterior teeth and buccal inclination (increased curve of Wilson) in the left maxilla.  
Severe left TMJ pain. 
Severe headaches. 
Myofascial pain. 
Significantly decreased oropharyngeal airway.
Difficulties swallowing. 
Hypertrophied right turbinate. 
Partial nasal airway obstruction. 
External nasal deformities secondary to previous nasal trauma (x2).
Anterior deep bite of 4 mm.

Recommended treatment would be as follows:

Presurgical orthodontics.  
Align and level maxillary arch.  
Align and level mandibular arch.  Slenderize the lower anterior teeth removing about 1.5 mm of tooth structure to correct for the tooth size discrepancy.   Try to upright the posterior teeth on the left side decreasing the accentuated curve of Wilson.
CT scan of TMs and jaws (TMJ Concepts procotol).
Surgery.
Left TMJ reconstruction and mandibular advancement with TMJ Concepts total joint prostheses.  
Left TMJ fat graft (harvest from the abdomen).
Left coronoidotomy.
Right TMJ articular disc repositioning and ligament repair with Mitek anchor.
Right mandibular sagittal split osteotomy.
Multiple maxillary osteotomies to transversely level the arch and advance both the maxilla and mandibular in a counterclockwise direction.  
Right inferior turbinectomy.
4.   Postsurgical orthodontics to refine occlusion.
