RADIOGRAPHIC EVALUATION:
Panorex shows the absence of all four third molars and maxillary second molars.  The lower arch is also missing the mandibular left first bicuspid.  She has severe arthritis in both TMJs with flattening and loss of vertical volume.  The sinuses look reasonably clear.  She has crowns on most of her maxillary teeth and some of her posterior teeth on the mandible.  

Waters view shows that the septum is fairly straight and turbinates are moderate in size.  The sinuses look relatively clear.  

Left TMJ tomogram shows significant arthritis is present.  Articular eminence is moderately steep.  The condyle is actually postured forward in the fossa.

Right TMJ tomogram shows a condyle with arthritic changes.  Articular eminence is moderately steep.  There appears to be decreased vertical and posterior joint space.  On opening, there is fairly good translation of both joints.  

Lateral cephalometric radiograph shows that she has a Class II skeletal and occlusal relationship. Slight increased occlusal plane angle.  Oropharyngeal measures about only 6 mm at the base of the tongue but up at the soft palate is measures 11 mm.  

Dental analysis shows that she has about a 3 to 4 mm vertical overbite relationship and about a 3 to 4 mm overjet relationship.  There is crowding in the lower anterior arch.  She has a missing mandibular first bicuspid but has the rest of the lower teeth.  The site of the maxillary right central incisor matches with the midline of the maxillary teeth.  The left mandibular cuspid is probably a good 3 to 4 mm posterior to the right cuspid. 

BASIC DIAGNOSES: 
Bilateral TMJ severe arthritis.
AP mandibular deficiency. 
Crowding of lower anterior arch. 
Class II occlusion on the left side and Class I on the right side.
TMJ pain. 
Myofascial pain.

RECOMMENDED TREATMENT: 
Extract mandibular right first bicuspid. 
Presurgical orthodontics.
Align and level maxillary arch.
Align and level mandibular arch. 
CT scan (TMJ Concepts protocol.)

Bilateral TMJ reconstruction and mandibular advancement with TMJ Concepts total joint prostheses.
