Patient History

Victor has had some previous surgeries including on his toenails, hernia repair, and some major bowel surgery. He also has had urethra and kidney surgery. He had some oral surgery for exposure of a tooth to try to pull it down into position. He has had problems in the past with asthma but not much problem with that now. 

Victor has been aware of having jaw joint problems for about a year though he has been told it may have been present for longer than that. The etiology is really unknown except the onset he feels was related to his work doing construction. In the past, he has had a history of headaches, neck, shoulder, back pain, TMJ pain, clicking and popping, etc. He has been in a splint for six months now, and he has also had physical therapy for six months. He takes no medications. He has no earaches, tinnitus, or vertigo. Other joints that bother him are occasionally his knees, ankles, toes, and elbows. 

He currently rates his headaches at a level of 2. They are predominantly in the frontal and temple area. Prior to receiving the splint, his headaches were 8 to 10. TMJ pain currently is about 3 but before his splint was about 8. Overall pain now is about 2 but prior to the splint was a 9. With his splint, his jaw function is a 0, diet at 1, disability at 6.

Clinical Evaluation

In frontal view, Victor has fairly good left-to-right facial symmetry. Upper lip length is about 20 mm. Upper tooth-to-lip relationship is about 1. Maxillary and mandibular dental midlines and chin midline are all pretty much on the facial midline. Victor has fairly good balance between the upper lip, cheeks, and nose. Mandible appears prominent. Chin-neckline is a little bit long. Chin-neck angle is well defined. Could not handle more than 1 to 2 mm of maxillary advancement.

Intraorally, Victor has all of his teeth back through his third molars present. He has some mild crowding, lower anterior arch. Has adequate attached gingiva. In the upper arch, he has all of his teeth back through the third molars. Has a full Class III cuspid relationship bilaterally. Has anterior and bilateral posterior cross-bites. Has a reverse anterior deep-bite of 3 mm. He probably has a good 10 mm AP discrepancy between the maxilla and mandible. Oropharynx appears clear. He does have a hyperplastic uvula. Does have a history of snoring. 

Intranasally, he does have a deviated nasal septum off towards the left side. Turbinates appear hyperplastic on the left side only, and this is probably causing him some significant airway obstruction, particularly on the left side.

To palpation, he has some mild tenderness over the styloid areas and SCMs and also some mild tenderness over the right TMJ. Joints seem to function relatively smoothly. Maximal incisal opening is about 60 mm, excursive movement to the right 8 mm, and to the left 9 mm. Joints seem to function relatively smoothly at this time.

Radiographic Evaluation

Panorex shows the presence of all the teeth. Sinuses look reasonably clear as best they can be seen. Left nasal airway looks more obliterated as compared to the right side. 

Right TMJ tomograms reveal the condyle is hard to see but can see it in one view. There is a decreased joint space but difficult to tell. The styloid process is prominent. Another x-ray shows the condyle has been postured forward in the fossa. Slightly increased posterior joint space. 

Left TMJ tomogram shows some flattening of the anterior head of the condyle. Moderate steepness of the articular eminence. Joint space does look a little bit increased. Open views show good translation of both condyles. 

Waters view shows what appears to be a pretty reasonable nasal airway. 

Cephalometric evaluation shows good oropharyngeal airway and Class III skeletal relationship. Slight transverse cant of occlusion with the right side being about 1 mm to 1.5 mm higher than the left side. In a Class I position, Victor still has posterior crossbite tendencies. Because of the tooth-size discrepancy with a small upper left lateral incisor, it is difficult to get the midline lined up because the upper right lateral incisor is larger than normal and the left one is smaller.

Headaches.
TMJ pain.

Recommended Treatment

Remove splint to see if TMJ symptoms recur

If TMJ symptoms recur, get MRI before reapplying splint

Presurgical orthodontics
Align and level maxillary arch. Reduce the size of the maxillary right lateral incisor and open up space around the left lateral incisor. Include second molars in the orthodontic appliances. Tip lateral incisor roots mesially and cuspid roots distally to make room for interdental osteotomies.
Align and level mandibular arch. Consider stripping lower anterior teeth, removing approximately 1.5 mm of tooth structure to keep from flaring them forward and to correct for the tooth-size discrepancy.

Reassess facial growth and development to determine if condylar hyperplasia is active

Surgery
Bilateral TMJ high condylectomies and articular disc repositioning with ligament repair with Mitek anchors.
Bilateral mandibular ramus osteotomies to set the mandible posteriorly.
Multiple maxillary osteotomies to advance it slightly but transversely expand it.
Left partial inferior turbinectomy.
Anterior mandibular horizontal osteotomy.
Extract maxillary and mandibular third molars.

Postsurgical orthodontics to refine occlusion
