KATE HOLLENBECK								04-08-03


Patient History

Kate’s problems apparently began when she was a young teenager. She fortunately has not had any significant pain problems with her condition but has undergone significant condylar resorption and retrusion of the mandible. She occludes now only on her second molars.

Kate has been under orthodontic treatment for some time, and apparently her occlusion is fairly well aligned to proceed on with surgery. 

Basic Diagnoses

Based on available records, which include the MRI, photographs, cephalometric tracing, as well as reports from other physicians, her diagnoses are as follows:

Bilateral TMJ idiopathic condylar resorption
Posterior vertical maxillary deficiency
AP mandibular deficiency
Transverse maxillary deficiency
Anterior open bite
Occluding only on the second molars
Headaches 
Myofascial pain
Decreased oropharyngeal airway
Class II occlusion
High occlusal plane angulation

Recommended Treatment

Presurgical orthodontics
Continue to align and level maxillary arch
Continue to align and level mandibular arch

Surgery
Bilateral TMJ condylectomies, meniscectomies, debridement, and reconstruction with TMJ Concepts total joint prostheses
Multiple maxillary osteotomies to expand the maxilla, downgraft the posterior aspect, and level the occlusal plane
Bilateral mandibular coronoidectomies
Bilateral TMJ fat grafts (harvested from abdomen)

Postsurgical orthodontics to refine occlusion

This plan should get her the best possible functional and aesthetic result.
