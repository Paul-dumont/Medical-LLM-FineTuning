RECOMMENDED TREATMENT
MRI of left and right TMJs. 
Presurgical orthodontics.
Align and level maxillary arch.  Tip lateral incisor roots mesially and cuspid roots distally to make room for interdental osteotomies.  Place the vertical step in the arch wire so that the 4 incisors are elevated and aligned at a level approximately 2 to 3 mm higher than the cuspids and posterior teeth.  Try to vertically level the posterior 5 teeth on each side. 
Align and level mandibular arch.  Slenderize the lower incisors removing approximately 2 mm of tooth structure to correct the tooth size discrepancy and to keep from tipping the incisors any further forward. 
Try to depress the left second bicuspid as it is elevated above the adjacent teeth.
Surgery.
Bilateral mandibular high condylectomies. 
Bilateral TMJ articular disc repositioning and ligament repair with Mitek anchors.
Bilateral mandibular ramus osteotomies to reposition the mandible posteriorly and decrease the occlusal plane angle.
Multiple maxillary osteotomies to advance it forward slightly and down graft it posteriorly.
