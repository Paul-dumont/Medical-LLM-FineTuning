MARIE ODOM									06/27/2007

Marie is 65 years old referred by Dan Clifford, DDS, 7520 Montgomery N.E. Bldg D, Ste. #2, Albuquerque, NM.  Marie reminded me that she had come to see me about 22 years ago for a second opinion.  Her history involves developing TMJ problems when she was about 19 years old following removal of her tonsils.  This was in 1960.  Her condition progressed on and then finally in 1985 she had bilateral TMJ surgery that included placement of either Proplast Teflon implants in her joints versus Silastic.  She developed worsening problems and in 1987 those implants were removed and bilateral TMJ dermal grafts were placed.  These have not done well.  She has been in splint therapy since the late 80s and currently still wears a splint.  She has been through physical therapy, acupuncture, and craniosacral therapy.  

In 1979, Marie was in an auto accident and had a severe whiplash injury that has caused some of the damage to her neck area.  Also, when she was 19, she was living with her aunt and the aunt had closed the door that normally was not closed and Marie was running through the house at night and ran into the door with her face and actually knocked the door out of the frame. 

CURRENT MEDICATIONS
Excedrin 2 to 3 per day.

Coreg 3.125 mg q.h.s.
Lexapro 10 mg q.h.s.
Nutrilite Double X supplement.

MEDICAL CONDITIONS:

Digestive problems. 

PAST HISTORY/PREVIOUS SURGERIES:  Significant bout of pneumonia 1944.  She had her tonsils removed in 1960, hysterectomy in 1972, previously mentioned TMJ surgeries and then gallbladder removed in 2000.

Pain in both eyes.
Loss of chewing abilities. 
Loss of voice and singing abilities.

Equilibrium problems.

Marie has some significant snoring problems, daytime somnolence and sleep apnea.  She has had a sleep study but she is not sure how accurate it was at the time because of some equipment failure.  She does have a CPAP machine.  She has difficulty sleeping at night that is moderate to severe but this is dependent on the level of pain that she has.  She does wake up occasionally at night unable to catch her breath.  She does have high blood pressure but she does not smoke.  

Marie rates her headaches at a level of 10 and these occur in the forehead, temple area and back of the head as well as she gets pain through her eyes.  She rates her TMJ pain at 10, average daily pain at 10, jaw function at 8, diet at 9 and disability at 9.  Marie does get earaches on the left side.  She gets occasional tinnitus and she gets frequent vertigo.  Other joints that bother her include her right shoulder, right knee, hips, SI joint and spine.  She also has problems with her left elbow.  She is receiving cold laser treatments.  She had significant trigger point injections, probably 100s of them.  Marie has generalized pain throughout her body probably related to fibromyalgia.  She does clench some at night. 

In frontal view, Marie has fairly good left-to-right facial symmetry.  Upper lip length is about 20 mm.  Upper tooth-to-lip relationship is –1.  Maxillary midline and mandibular dental midline is pretty much on the facial midline.  Chin may be just slightly to the right side maybe a couple of mm.  In profile view, Marie has some slight AP maxillary deficiency maybe 2 to 3 mm.  Chin appears retruded by probably 10 to 12 mm.  Intraorally, Marie has her lower teeth back through the second molars present.  She has some moderate crowding in the lower anterior arch.  She has adequate attached gingiva.  In the upper arch she has all of her teeth back through the second molars as well.  Basically a Class I cuspid molar relationship.  She has about a 2 mm vertical overbite relationship.  The uvula is a little bit elongated.  Soft palate appears elongated.  Faucial pillars may be moderately constricted.  Tonsils are gone.  Intranasally, turbinates appear moderate in size.  The septum is deviated a little bit off towards the left side.  Airway, however, appears adequate.  Sometimes during the day other people will catch Marie holding her breath and they will have to remind her to breathe.  If Marie tries to eat anything she gets pain line a bone shooting up through her skull.  Maximal incisal opening is 39 mm but she can open to about 30 mm without severe pain.  Excursive movements are about 3 mm in each direction.  To palpation she has high moderate to severe pain around the masseters, TMJs, styloid areas, moderate pain around the medial pterygoids, posterior neck and shoulder areas and mild tenderness over the SCMs and temporalis area.  Marie has had rhizotomies to some of the cranial nerves in the back of her neck and this has helped her pain through her neck and shoulder areas.  This was done only on the right side.

RECOMMENDED TREATMENT
Consider evaluation at the Environmental Center of Dallas to rule out hypersensitivities to materials to be used in the prostheses including possible material samples implanted into the forearm.
CT scans of jaws and jaw joints (TMJ Concepts protocol.)
Surgery.
Bilateral TMJ reconstruction with TMJ Concepts total joint prostheses.
Bilateral TMJ fat grafts (harvest from the abdomen.)
Application and removal of arch bars.
Postsurgical physical therapy as necessary to maximize jaw function.
Postsurgical orthodontics to refine occlusion.
