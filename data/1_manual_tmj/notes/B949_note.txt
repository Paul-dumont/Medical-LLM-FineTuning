JEFFREY MOODY									08/09/2008

Jeffrey is 15 years old.  His primary concerns are with his facial balance and extremely strong chin.  The orthodontist is Jim L. Caskey, DDS, MS, 210 E. Lamberth Road, Sherman, TX 75092. 

Basic diagnoses for Jeffrey would be as follows:    

AP maxillary deficiency.
AP mandibular deficiency. 
AP macrogenia of both bony and soft tissue.
Significant lingual inclination of the lower incisors.
Low occlusal plane angulation. 
Class I occlusion.
Moderate anterior deep bite of about 4 mm.
Moderate decreased oropharyngeal airway.
 
Recommended treatment would be as follows:

Presurgical orthodontics.  
Align and level maxillary arch.  Flare up her incisors forward about 2 mm creating space between the upper laterals and cuspids.  Tip lateral incisor roots mesially and cuspid roots distally.
Align and level mandibular arch.  Try to increase the lower incisor angulation.
Surgery.
Maxillary osteotomies to advance it forward, remove the spacing between the upper laterals and cuspids, remove the posterior maxilla superiorly approximately 4 mm.  
Bilateral mandibular ramus osteotomies to advance the mandible in a clockwise direction, moving the posterior aspect superiorly and decreasing the deep bite. 
Reduce soft tissue thickness in the chin area.  
3.   Postsurgical orthodontics to refine occlusion.
