Clinical Examination

Upper lip length in about 21 mm. Upper tooth-to-lip relationship is almost 4 mm. Maxillary dental midline is pretty much on the facial midline. Mandibular dental midline is fairly close to being on the facial midline, and chin is fairly close or could be just slightly off to the right side about a millimeter. Kathy has some slight AP maxillary deficiency of probably about 2 to 3 mm. Chin is retruded probably about 10 to 15 mm. Chin-neckline is a little bit short, and she has some fullness in the submental area. Chin-neck angle is reasonably well-defined. As Kathy postures her mandible forward, it does significantly improve her overall facial profile. 
Kathy has all of her lower teeth back through the second molars present. She has braces on back through the first molars. Has adequate attached gingiva around the lower anterior arch. Lower teeth are fairly well aligned and leveled. Lower incisors might be depressed just a little bit below the level of the cuspids by 1 to 2 mm.

In the upper arch, she has all of her teeth back through the second molars. She has a significant reverse curve of occlusion in the upper arch. She has about a 5-mm Class II occlusion on the right side and about a 4 mm on the left side. She has anterior deep-bite of probably about 5 mm. Transversely, the occlusal plane is fairly level. Cuspids are fairly symmetric anteroposteriorly. Oropharynx appears relatively clear, although it was a little bit difficult to visualize as it should be. She has lingual tori that are fairly good size. 

Intranasally, Kathy has hypertrophied turbinates. Turbinates look quite large particularly on the right side. The left side appears to touch against the septum. The septum is deviated a little bit off to the left side. Probably has some significant airway obstruction. Kathy is not sure if she breathes through her mouth or her nose when she sleeps.

To palpation, Kathy has no tenderness around the head and neck area. No tenderness over the TMJs. However, on opening she has clicks in both joints at about 10 mm and closing clicks at about 5 mm. 

Incisal opening is about 45 mm. Excursive movement to the right is 12 mm and to the left 10 mm.

She has all of her teeth except for the four third molars which are absent. She has a root canal on the maxillary left central incisor. The right condyle appears somewhat arthritis with flattening on the anterior aspect and some anterior beaking. Left joint appears a little flattened and may have a small osteophyte on the top of it. Posterior teeth appear to be a little bit flattened without much prominence of the cusps on all the molars.

Waters view shows that the sinuses appear quite clear. Some deviation of the septum in the bony area off to the left side. Turbinates appear quite large and appear to be obliterating most of the functional airway. This might be the source of some of her chronic rhinitis. 

Left TMJ tomogram shows a condyle with some significant flattening on the anterior head and appears to be some sclerosis on top of the head of the condyle. Condyle might be slightly posteriorly positioned in the fossa. Articular eminence is moderately steep. Some anterior beaking is present. Appears to be fairly large air cell above the posterior aspect of the fossa.

Right TMJ tomogram shows a condyle that has a hockey stick shape to it. Quite a bite of flattening on the anterior head, and some anterior beak is present. Appears to be some peaking of head of the condyle. Articular eminence is moderately steep. Joint space looks fairly well-balanced around the condyle.

On opening, there is good translation of the condyles bilaterally. 

Cephalometric radiograph shows there is a skeletal Class II jaw relationship and Class II occlusal relationship. Oropharyngeal airway is quite constricted and in one area appears to be only perhaps 3 mm at the most. That is opposite the soft palate. At the base of the tongue, it is closer to about 8 mm.

Recommended Treatment

Presurgical orthodontics 
Continue to align and level maxillary arch. Open up a millimeter of space around each of the upper lateral incisors to help account for the tooth-size discrepancy. Be sure to keep lateral incisor roots tipped mesially and cuspid roots tipped distally
Continue to align and level mandibular arch

Surgery
Multiple maxillary osteotomies to move the anterior maxilla superiorly, posterior aspect inferiorly at the level of the occlusal plane, and level the reverse curve of Spee
Bilateral mandibular ramus osteotomies to advance the mandible in a counterclockwise direction
Bilateral TMJ articular disc repositioning and ligament repair with Mitek anchors 
Partial turbinectomies
Nasal septoplasty

Postsurgical orthodontics to refine occlusion 

Restorative dentistry to build up upper lateral incisors and also to define the mandibular incisal edges
