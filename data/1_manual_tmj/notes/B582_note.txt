JEAN HUFF										05/14/2008

Jean is 72 years old, referred by Dr. David Banta, 6140 Sherry Lane, Dallas, TX 75225.  On approximately February 18, 2008, or February 20, 2008, Jean had a mandibular right horizontally impacted third molar removed as it was beginning to cause her problems and was affecting her other teeth.  Since the tooth was removed, the area has remained fairly numb and it is now hypersensitive to touch in the lower lip and chin area and her teeth on that side, particularly her front teeth, have become somewhat hypersensitive.  She is not aware of any significant improvement since the incident.  Jean is on the following medications:

Basic diagnoses for Jena would be as follows:
Neuropraxia versus neurotmesis of the right inferior alveolar nerve following surgical removal of mandibular right third molar. 
Paresthesia to right lower lip, chin, teeth, and gingiva. 

Recommended treatment would be as follows:

Continue to observe for an additional 3 months to see if there is any improvement in her sensibility to the lower right lip, chin, teeth, and gums.
If no significant improvement, consider right inferior alveolar nerve exploration and repair, if indicated.
