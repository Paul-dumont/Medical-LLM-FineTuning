STEPHANIE SALMON								10/25/2005

Stephanie is 20 years old, referred by Dr. Hilton Goldreich, Plano, Texas.  Stephanie has been diagnosed as having Treacher-Collins syndrome.  She has had previous orthodontics from the age of 7 to 9.  

CURRENT MEDICATIONS
1.  Cymbalta 60 mg q.d.
2.  Bactrim DS q.d.

She has had multiple reconstructions around her eyes, cheeks and nose by Dr. Jeff Fearon.  She has probably had about 15 procedures performed so far.  She has had no work done on her jaws.  Besides facial reconstruction, she has had her tonsils and adenoids removed at age 6.  She has a Baha hearing aide.   She also has been diagnosed as having scoliosis and she has had gallstones removed.  

PRIMARY CONCERNS
1.  Teeth and jaws do not fit together.
2.  Only a couple of her teeth meet on either side.
3.  Sleep apnea.
4.  Difficulty breathing.
5.  Severe snoring.
6.  Difficulties eating and chewing.
7.  Facial imbalance. 
8.  Unhappy with ear reconstruction. 
9.  Jaw joints click and pop.
10. Nasal airway obstruction. 
11. Predominately mouth breather. 

Stephanie apparently has no problems with earaches, tinnitus, vertigo and is not aware of any clenching or bruxism.  She does have profound hearing loss as she has no ear canals and part of her middle ears is absent.  She also has some nerve damage.  

In frontal view, Stephanie has some significant deficiencies in the upper orbital area.  Nasal bridge is a little bit wide.  Nose deviates a little bit off to the right side and then back towards the left.  Upper lip length is about 18 mm.  Upper tooth-to-lip relationship is about 3 mm.  There is some depression in the bitemporal areas towards the anterior aspect.  Palpebral fissures are narrow with the left side being more narrow than the right side.  Right pupil is dilated compared to the left one.  Right eye is about 4 to 5 mm higher than the left eye.  Maxillary midline is about 3 mm off to the left and mandibular dental midline is about 2 mm off to the right.  Mandibular dental midline and chin are fairly well aligned with each other.  The chin also is a couple of mm off to the right side.  There is about 1 mm vertical reverse overbite.  In profile view, the maxillary is retruded about 3 to 4 mm.  Lower lip is reverting.  Chin is probably 8 to 10 mm AP deficient.  She has lip incompetence of probably about 10 mm.  Chin neckline is short.  She has a head postured forward neck position.  Intraorally, she has all of her lower teeth back through the second molar.  Some severe crowding in the lower anterior arch.

RECOMMENDED TREATMENT
MRI of left and right TMJs.
Surgery stage 1.
Extract 4 impacted third molars.
Extract mandibular second bicuspids, maxillary left second bicuspid and right first bicuspid.
Presurgical orthodontics.
Align and level maxillary arch.  Shift maxillary midline 3 mm to the right to the facial midline.
Align and level mandibular arch.
CT scan with 3-D modeling to aide in jaw reconstruction. 
Surgery stage 2.
Multiple maxillary osteotomies to advance, down graft the posterior aspect, correct any transverse arch discrepancies.
Bilateral mandibular ramus osteotomies to counterclockwise rotate the mandible tipping the incisors posteriorly and advancing the chin.
Anterior mandibular horizontal osteotomy to advance the inferior border of the mandible and augment the chin.
Nasoseptoplasty. 
Turbinectomy.
Postsurgical orthodontics to refine occlusion.
Surgery stage 3.
Bilateral infraorbital reconstruction.
Bilateral eyelid and canthal tendon reconstruction.
Consider external rhinoplasty.
Reevaluate ears (consult with Dr. Steve Parel and Dr. Gil Triplett)
Surgery stage 4.
Osseointegrated implants in the temple area.
Construction of prosthetic ears.
