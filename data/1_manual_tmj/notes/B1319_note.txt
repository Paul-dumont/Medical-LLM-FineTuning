MAGGIE SNEED								10/24/2011

Maggie is 64 years old.  She has a past history of breast cancer.  She presents today with a history of lump on the roof of her mouth with an associated ulcer that has been present for about 6 weeks.  She mentioned the pain was quite significant and has decreased some but still is painful for her.  Examination shows that she has a palatal torus present.  It is hard and firm.  The size of the tori is probably roughly about 15 to 20 mm in length and about 8 to 10 mm width.  Towards the posterior aspect of the tori is an ulcerated area that is tender to touch.  She has mandibular tori as well that are nonpainful.  I told her that I do not believe there is any malignancy present, but I have recommended that she have the ulcer and the tori removed and histologically evaluated.  I am referring her to the dental school for that treatment.  

She reports hoarseness in her throat.  This has been present since the summertime and she wakes hoarse and has to clear her throat for most of the morning.  It does get better during the day.  She has not experienced this situation before to this extent.  I gave her the name of Dr. Larry Weprin, an ENT specialist, to evaluate her vocal cords.  I gave her his name, address and phone number.  

Maggie is certainly a delightful lady and hopefully will follow up with the recommendations.  

Diagnoses:

Palatal torus.
Ulceration on the posterior aspect of the palatal torus of 6 weeks’ duration.
Hoarseness.

Recommendations:

Referral to dental school for removal of the palatal torus and ulcerated lesion.  
Consultation with Dr. Larry Weprin to evaluate the chronic hoarseness.
