Recommended treatment would be as follows:
Presurgical orthodontics. 
Align and level maxillary arch.  Tip lateral incisor roots mesially and cuspid roots distally to make room for interdental osteotomies.  Place the vertical step in the arch wire so that the 4 incisors are elevated at a level approximately 2 to 3 mm higher than the cuspids and posterior teeth.  Try to level the posterior 5 teeth on each side. 
Align and level mandibular arch.  Try to elevate the lower incisors slightly and depress the bicuspids as there is a slightly reverse of occlusion in the lower arch.  
Surgery.
Bilateral mandibular high condylectomies. 
Bilateral TMJ articular disc repositioning and ligament repair with Mitek anchors.
Bilateral mandibular ramus osteotomies to reposition the mandible posteriorly and decrease the occlusal plane angle.
Multiple maxillary osteotomies to advance it forward slightly and down graft it posteriorly.  
Extract malaligned maxillary mandibular third molars.
Uvulectomy.
Bilateral infraorbital/malar augmentations.
Grafting intraorbital and malar area.
Postsurgical orthodontics to refine occlusion.
