RADIOGRAPHIC EVALUATION
Panorex taken last week at Dr. XXXX's office shows that the third molars are absent. She has a 3 x 3 retainer. Sinuses look reasonably healthy. She has a little bit of adenoid tissue still remaining in the nasopharynx, but does not appear to be causing any nasal airway obstruction.

Basic diagnoses for B143 would be as follows:
Bilateral TMJ severe condylar resorption and articular disc anteriorly dislocated with severe degenerative changes rendering them nonsalvageable. Severe anterior mandibular hypoplasia.
AP maxillary hypoplasia and posterior vertical hypoplasia.
Anterior maxillary vertical hyperplasia.
AP microgenia. Anterior mandibular excessive height. High occlusal plane angle. Class II occlusion.
Anterior open bite of about 2 to 3 mm.
Accentuated curve of Spee in the upper arch and a reversed accentuated curve of Spee in the lower arch.

Recommended treatment would be as follows:
Presurgical orthodontics. Align and level maxillary arch. In the upper arch, it would be helpful to align the teeth to 2 different occlusal plane levels by placing a small step in the upper arch wire between the cuspids and laterals to elevate the four incisors in a slightly elevated plane. Tip the lateral incisor roots mesially and cuspid roots distally to make room for interdental osteotomies. Align and level mandibular arch. Try to extrude the lower 4 incisors vertically about 1 to 2 mm to help correct the reversed curve of Spee. CT scan of jaws and jaw joints (TMJ Concepts protocol).
Surgery.
Bilateral TMJ reconstruction and mandibular advancement with TMJ Concepts total joint prostheses. Multiple maxillary osteotomies to move the anterior aspect superiorly, posterior aspect inferiorly, as well as correct any vertical discrepancies and transverse discrepancies that may be present. Fat graft to bilateral TMJs (harvest from the abdomen).
Anterior mandibular vertical reduction and AP augmentation. Left nasal partial turbinectomy.
4. Postsurgical orthodontics to refine occlusion.
