Recommended treatment would be as follows:
1. MRI of left and right TMJ.
2. Presurgical orthodontics. a.	Continue to align and level maxillary arch. Leave the maxillary left second bicuspid elevated if it is anklyosed, otherwise try to extrude it down into the occlusal plane.
b.	Continue to align and level mandibular arch. Consider opening up space in the left mandibular quadrant perhaps between the bicuspids to push the anterior teeth forward on the left side shifting the midline to the right at least 3 mm and distalizing the posterior teeth. If the space opens up to equal the size of a bicuspid, then eventually an osteointegrated implant can be placed. If not, then the space would be surgically removed at the time of the orthognathic surgery. Tip first bicuspid root mesially and second bicuspid root distally. c.	Consider building up the lower incisors to the height desired by the dentist with temporary crowns so that we can achieve the best possible treatment outcome and dental alignment.
3. Surgery. a.	Right TMJ articular disc repositioning and ligament repair with Mitek anchor. b.	Multiple maxillary osteotomies to advance it slightly but down graft it 4 to 5 mm. c.	Bilateral mandibular ramus osteotomies to advance it forward. d.	Anterior mandibular horizontal osteotomy to increase the vertical height and AP position of the chin. e.	Nasal septoplasty. f.	Partial left inferior turbinectomy. g.	Left body ostectomy if there is not enough room to place a dental implant and crown.
4. Postsurgical orthodontics to refine occlusion.
5. Restorative dentistry to place an implant and a crown in the space created between the bicuspids and finalize the rest of the restorative work that is necessary.
